a summary of the relevent coursework i've completed

CS111 - Intro to Computer Science I

In my Introduction to Computer Science course (CS111), I developed "computational problem-solving skills" through programming in Python. We covered fundamental concepts early on, such as object-oriented programming and recursion. These concepts were applied through increasingly complex weekly problem sets, leaving me with a much stronger foundation in programming than when I started.

Difficulty: 5/10

Grade: B+

CS112 - Intro to Computer Science II

In my Data Structures and Algorithms course (CS112), we built upon the foundations of object-oriented programming and recursion, this time using Java. We then continued to cover many topics apart of DSA incuding algorithm analysis, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, heaps, and hashing. We also covered a variety of algorithms for searching and sorting. The course emphasized the practical application of Data Structures and Algorithm concepts to solve complex computational problems efficiently.

Difficulty: 7/10

Grade: B+

CS131 - Discrete Math/Combinatoric Structures

In my Discrete Mathematics course (CS131), we focused on representing and analyzing combinatorial structures commonly used in computer science. We primarily applied what we learned in the format of formal proofs, which emphasized rigorous reasoning. The course heavily featured proofs and logical reasoning, both essential for proving the correctness and efficiency of algorithms.

Difficulty: 8/10

Grade: B+
