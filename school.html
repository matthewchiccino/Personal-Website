CS112 - Intro to Computer Science II

In my Data Structures and Algorithms course (CS112), we built upon the foundations of object-oriented programming, this time using Java. The course was an in-depth exploration of advanced programming techniques and data structures. Key topics included recursion, algorithm analysis, and a range of data structures such as linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, and tables. We also covered fundamental algorithms for searching and sorting. The course emphasized the practical application of these concepts to solve complex computational problems efficiently.

Difficulty: 7/10

Grade: B+

In my Discrete Mathematics course, I focused on representing and analyzing combinatoric structures commonly used in computer science. We delved into various techniques and principles for manipulating these structures, with a strong emphasis on rigorous reasoning. The course heavily featured proofs and logical reasoning, which are essential for proving the correctness and efficiency of algorithms.

Difficulty: 8/10

Grade: B+
